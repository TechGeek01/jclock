jClock

This little clock can be used as both a 12- or 24-hour display, as well as a trigger for timed events on your website.

Implementation

To implement this script, simply stick <script type="text/javascript" src="https://raw.github.com/TechGeek01/jclock/master/jclock.js" \> into the header of your webpage, change your <body> tag's onload attribute to setDate();, and you're good to go!

Usage

To use jClock, you can interact with the script in one of two ways.

Display the clock

Set the id attribute of a container to either 12clock (a 12-hour clock), or 24clock (24-hour clock), and jClock does the rest.

Trigger timed events

If your site has JavaScript-handled events, you can trigger them with jClock simply by nesting your action within an if statement, and using one or more of the variables month, date, h24, m, or s as the trigger.
